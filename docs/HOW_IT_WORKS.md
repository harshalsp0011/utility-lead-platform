# How the Utility Lead Platform Works
### A Plain-English Guide for Business Stakeholders

---

## What Problem Does This Solve?

Finding companies that overpay on utilities, researching them, writing a personalized email, following up multiple times, and tracking whether they responded — all of this takes hours per lead when done manually.

This platform does all of that automatically. A consultant's job shrinks down to two simple decisions:

1. **"Is this company worth reaching out to?"** — approve or skip
2. **"Is this email good enough to send?"** — approve or edit

Everything else — finding the company, researching it, writing the email, following up, tracking replies — happens on its own.

---

## The Journey of a Lead

### Step 1 — The System Finds a Company

The platform reads business news every day looking for companies that are likely spending a lot on utilities. It looks for signals like:

- A hospital opening a new wing
- A manufacturer scaling up production
- A company moving into a large new facility
- A business operating across multiple locations

When it spots one of these signals, it saves the company automatically. Nothing needs to be done manually.

**You can also add leads yourself** — either by importing your existing contact list from HubSpot, or by typing in a company name and contact directly. All three paths lead to the same place.

---

### Step 2 — The System Researches the Company

Once a company is in the system, a research assistant (the Analyst) goes to work:

- It looks up who works at the company and finds the right person to contact — typically a VP of Operations, Facilities Manager, or CFO
- It estimates how much that company likely spends on utilities and how much could be saved
- It gives the company a score from 0 to 100 based on how good a fit they are

Companies that score high are ones with many locations, large workforces, energy-intensive industries (like healthcare or manufacturing), or are in states where they can switch energy suppliers.

---

### Step 3 — A Human Decides Whether to Pursue

The scored companies appear on the **Leads page** in the dashboard. A consultant reviews them and makes a simple call:

- **Approve** — yes, reach out to this company
- **Skip** — not a good fit right now

High-scoring leads can be approved in bulk with one click. Lower-scoring ones can be reviewed individually.

Nothing moves forward until a human says yes.

---

### Step 4 — The System Writes a Personalized Email

For every approved company, the platform writes a custom outreach email. This is not a template with blanks filled in — it's a genuinely written email that:

- Mentions the company by name
- References what the system learned about them (their industry, their size, why they're a good fit)
- Leads with a specific savings estimate in dollars
- Makes one clear ask — a call, a quick reply, or a free audit

After writing the email, a second AI review checks it on five things: Is it personalized? Does it mention a savings figure? Does it ask for something specific? Does it sound human? Is the subject line compelling?

If the email doesn't pass that review, the system rewrites it. This loop can happen up to twice before the email is saved for human review.

Over time, the platform learns which types of emails get replies in which industries and naturally shifts toward what works.

---

### Step 5 — A Human Reviews and Approves the Email

Every drafted email lands in the **Email Review Queue**. A consultant opens each one and sees it exactly as the recipient will — with the From address, the To address, the subject line, and the full email body laid out like a real inbox.

From here the consultant can:

- **Send it as-is** — approve and it goes out
- **Edit it first** — change a word, adjust the tone, then approve
- **Reject it** — send it back to be rewritten from scratch

No email is ever sent without a human explicitly approving it. This is a firm rule.

---

### Step 6 — The Email Is Sent

Once approved, the email goes out automatically with open and click tracking enabled, so you can see if the recipient opened it or clicked a link. A standard unsubscribe option is added to the bottom of every email.

There is a daily cap to keep outreach at a professional, controlled pace rather than blasting contacts all at once.

---

### Step 7 — The System Follows Up Automatically

After the first email goes out, the platform automatically schedules three follow-up emails — no manual action needed:

| | When It Sends | Tone |
|---|---|---|
| Follow-up 1 | 3 days after the first email | A gentle "just checking in" |
| Follow-up 2 | 7 days after the first email | Restates the offer, includes a link to book a call |
| Follow-up 3 | 14 days after the first email | A polite "last touch" message |

Each follow-up is written by the same AI and is personalized in the same way as the first email — not a generic "just following up on my previous email."

If a reply comes in at any point, all remaining follow-ups are cancelled automatically. The system does not keep messaging someone who has already responded.

---

### Step 8 — Replies Are Detected and Tracked

When a prospect replies, HubSpot (the external sales CRM) picks up the incoming message and notifies this platform immediately. The platform then:

- Stops all scheduled follow-ups for that contact
- Marks the company as "Replied" in the pipeline
- Alerts the sales team so they can respond personally

If the prospect books a meeting through the scheduling link in Follow-up 2, the platform marks the company as "Meeting Booked" — the goal has been reached.

## Where Leads Come From

There are three ways a company enters the platform — all three get the same research, scoring, and outreach:

| Source | How it works |
|---|---|
| **Daily news scan** | Platform reads business news and identifies companies with high utility spend signals |
| **HubSpot import** | Pull in your existing contacts and accounts from HubSpot — they go through the same scoring and get a fresh, personalized email |
| **Manual entry** | A sales team member types in a company and contact directly — useful for referrals, conference contacts, or inbound leads |

---

## What Happens to Each Company Over Time

Every company in the system has a status that tells you exactly where it is in the process:

| Status | What it means |
|---|---|
| **New** | Just discovered — not yet researched |
| **Enriched** | Right contact found at the company |
| **Scored** | Research complete, score assigned |
| **Approved** | Human said "yes, reach out" |
| **Draft Ready** | Email written and waiting for your review |
| **Contacted** | Email sent |
| **Replied** | They wrote back |
| **Meeting Booked** | They scheduled a call |
| **Won** | Deal closed |
| **No Response** | All follow-ups sent, no reply |
| **Archived** | Not a good fit — removed from active pipeline |

---

## HubSpot — The Sales Team's View

HubSpot sits alongside this platform as a window for the sales team. The platform handles all the intelligence and automation. HubSpot provides:

- A familiar CRM view of the pipeline for anyone who lives in HubSpot
- Reply detection — when a prospect responds to an email, HubSpot catches it and notifies the platform
- Meeting scheduling — prospects can book a call through a HubSpot calendar link
- A record of all communication history per contact

Think of it this way: **this platform is the engine, HubSpot is the dashboard** the sales team watches.

| Task | Old Way | With This Platform |
|---|---|---|
| Finding leads | Manual research, LinkedIn, buying lists | Automatic daily scan of business news |
| Qualifying leads | Gut feel or spreadsheet | AI score 0–100 with written explanation |
| Finding the right contact | LinkedIn search, guessing | Automatic lookup from business database |
| Writing outreach emails | Hours per email | Written and reviewed by AI in minutes |
| Following up | Remembering manually, calendar reminders | Fully automatic, personalized, timed |
| Tracking replies | Email inbox, spreadsheet | Automatic, updates pipeline instantly |
| Managing the pipeline | CRM manual updates | Updates itself as events happen |

---

## What This Does NOT Do

- It does not send any email without a human approving it first
- It does not contact anyone who has unsubscribed
- It does not exceed the daily send limit
- It does not make the final sale — that conversation still happens person-to-person
- It does not replace the sales team's judgment — it just removes the time-consuming research and writing work so they can focus on conversations
